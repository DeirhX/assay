Order pipeline

How this works

This is the index for everything that may change the portfolio: deliberately planned trades, exact orders queued locally, and working orders accepted by IBKR. The three stores remain separate so a local “submitted” record is never mistaken for a broker fill. Use the execution bar above to build, review, preview, and place.

Build orders

How this works

Start from positions outside their target bands, edit the proposed amount, then choose shares or an eligible written-option route directly in that row. Preview the portfolio impact before adding anything to the queue. You can append the result or explicitly replace earlier Rebalance orders; separately planned Exit routes are retained. Use Clear amounts to start one additional trade from zero. Nothing is sent to IBKR here.

Optimizer

How this works

Advanced Targets mode: size the whole book from holdings + watchlist + topic discoveries under your constraints. Stages a draft for Composition review — never trades by itself.

Composition & draft

How this works

Set allocation-segment ratios, fold standalone names into sleeves, and review staged target-model changes before applying. Applying changes updates target bands; it does not trade or change current holdings. A backup is retained for reversal.

Review projected portfolio

How this works

Review the exact order queue before IBKR preview: if every queued order executes, where does the portfolio land? Every target and sleeve shows now versus after, together with cash, tax, funding, and position warnings. Suggestions are deliberately excluded here—only queued orders can pass this approval gate.

Advanced reductions & exits

How this works

For positions being reduced, compare tax-aware share tranches with executable covered-call routes. Adding a tranche or contract only updates the shared order queue; the projected portfolio must still be approved before IBKR preview, and placement always requires explicit confirmation.

Watchlist

How this works

A shortlist of tickers you're interested in, starred from anywhere — a deep-dive, a rebalance row, a strategy proposal. It's just interest, not a decision: nothing here is sized or committed. The next step turns your picks into a drafted rebalance plan (with LLM suggestions for names that would round it out).

Preview & place

How this works

Reconcile the approved order queue with IBKR, inspect margin and commission estimates, then confirm placement. This is the only step that can send real orders; trading is disabled by default and should be paper-tested first.

Portfolio risk lens

How this works

Single-name bands are blind to the real risk in a concentrated semis/AI book: the names move together. This shows correlation, effective number of independent bets, the share of variance that is pure co-movement, and a factor-shock stress test. It is a decision aid, not a risk oracle — read the caveats.

Process attribution

How this works

Every other lens measures a position; this measures the process. Over the window it compares your actual time-weighted return against two skill-free baselines: never rebalancing (freeze the book at the window start and let prices run) and the benchmark (put the same koruna into SPY/QQQ). Deposits are neutralized so a transfer never looks like alpha, and every foreign price is converted day-by-day so currency swings don't flatter or bury the result. Read the caveats — a thin ledger or a missing price makes a curve approximate.

Tax calendar

How this works

The Czech 3-year exemption is the one return with no market risk. This lays every lot on a forward calendar: taxable-gain lots approaching the mark (wait a few weeks and the gain is tax-free) and taxable-loss lots whose harvest window is closing (a realized loss stops offsetting gains once the lot turns exempt). Base currency; the clock is each lot's open date, never IBKR's ST/LT code. Analysis, not tax advice.

Decision journal

How this works

Log every decision with its thesis and price. Outcomes (your recorded price, or the live snapshot mark) calibrate whether your judgement actually works — a buy is "right" if it rose, a trim if it fell. Append-only; stored in your private data. Not advice.

Your sanitized IBKR positions, cash, and tax lots. Resync runs a read-only Flex pull — a point-in-time snapshot, not live prices. Weights are % of NAV from base-currency market value; refresh before acting on exact figures.

Portfolio history

How this works

Your entire executed-trade ledger and day-by-day portfolio value, reconstructed via read-only Flex one ≤365-day window at a time, back to account inception. The chart plots NAV over time with every buy (green) and sell (red) marked on the day it happened. Requires a Flex query that includes the Trades section and the Net Asset Value (NAV) in Base section (that one emits the daily NAV; not the same as “Change in NAV”). Set IBKR_FLEX_HISTORY_QUERY_ID. Cached privately — not committed.

Activity

How this works

A durable, newest-first log of the tickers you've opened and the background tasks that have finished (analyses, Deep Research, IBKR syncs, reviews).
