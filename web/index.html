Rebalance planner

How this works

Drift of every targeted name vs its no-trade band, with the minimal band-closing trade sized in CZK. Edit any planned amount to simulate — the cash math and projected weights update live. Weights are % of the invested book (market value ÷ invested), matching rebalance.py --check. This is advice, not orders. This is the manual path — you close drift one name at a time. The Plan tab is the guided, research-first route; the Optimizer resizes the whole book automatically.

Portfolio optimizer

How this works

One candidate pool — everything you hold, plus your basket picks and segment-discovered names — sized as a whole book by conviction under your constraints. Held names ride as carry, want picks grow, low-conviction and excluded names fund the plan. The result is a proposal you preview and stage into the working draft; nothing changes your live model until you commit there. This is the automated path — it sizes everything at once. The Plan tab is the guided, research-first route; the Rebalance planner is manual, one name at a time.

Working draft

How this works

A safe scratchpad for changes to your plan. Proposals from strategy runs and manual edits collect here so you can review everything together and apply it in one step. Commit makes it live (a backup is kept, so it's reversible); Discard throws it away. Pinned names you've chosen are never dropped without flagging it.

Target state

How this works

If you execute what's on the table, where does the book land? Every targeted name and sleeve drawn on its band track — a ghost tick for now, a solid tick for after — with the headline deltas (bands closed, cash vs its band, net cash, realized Czech tax) up top. Projects the staged basket when one exists, else the plan's suggested amounts. Read-only: nothing here stages or places anything.

Exit planner

How this works

A graceful way out of the names the rebalance planner wants to shrink: tax-optimal lot sequencing (Czech 3-year rule), a liquidity-aware multi-day scale-out with a suggested GTC limit ladder, and an options overlay (covered call / protective-put collar). Everything is advisory — "Stage tranche" only drops a slice into the Trade desk basket, which you still preview and place yourself. No option orders are ever placed.

Watchlist

How this works

A shortlist of tickers you're interested in, starred from anywhere — a deep-dive, a rebalance row, a strategy proposal. It's just interest, not a decision: nothing here is sized or committed. The next step turns your picks into a drafted rebalance plan (with LLM suggestions for names that would round it out).

Trade desk

How this works

Place the staged rebalance basket through Interactive Brokers via the local Client Portal Gateway. Every basket is previewed for margin and commission, and every order needs your explicit confirmation before it is sent. This is the only part of Assay that can place real trades — it is disabled by default and paper-first. Not advice; you are responsible for every order.

Portfolio risk lens

How this works

Single-name bands are blind to the real risk in a concentrated semis/AI book: the names move together. This shows correlation, effective number of independent bets, the share of variance that is pure co-movement, and a factor-shock stress test. It is a decision aid, not a risk oracle — read the caveats.

Process attribution

How this works

Every other lens measures a position; this measures the process. Over the window it compares your actual time-weighted return against two skill-free baselines: never rebalancing (freeze the book at the window start and let prices run) and the benchmark (put the same koruna into SPY/QQQ). Deposits are neutralized so a transfer never looks like alpha, and every foreign price is converted day-by-day so currency swings don't flatter or bury the result. Read the caveats — a thin ledger or a missing price makes a curve approximate.

Tax calendar

How this works

The Czech 3-year exemption is the one return with no market risk. This lays every lot on a forward calendar: taxable-gain lots approaching the mark (wait a few weeks and the gain is tax-free) and taxable-loss lots whose harvest window is closing (a realized loss stops offsetting gains once the lot turns exempt). Base currency; the clock is each lot's open date, never IBKR's ST/LT code. Analysis, not tax advice.

Decision journal

How this works

Log every decision with its thesis and price. Outcomes (your recorded price, or the live snapshot mark) calibrate whether your judgement actually works — a buy is "right" if it rose, a trim if it fell. Append-only; stored in your private data. Not advice.

Your sanitized IBKR positions, cash, and tax lots. Resync runs a read-only Flex pull — a point-in-time snapshot, not live prices. Weights are % of NAV from base-currency market value; refresh before acting on exact figures.

Portfolio history

How this works

Your entire executed-trade ledger and day-by-day portfolio value, reconstructed via read-only Flex one ≤365-day window at a time, back to account inception. The chart plots NAV over time with every buy (green) and sell (red) marked on the day it happened. Requires a Flex query that includes the Trades section and the Net Asset Value (NAV) in Base section (that one emits the daily NAV; not the same as “Change in NAV”). Set IBKR_FLEX_HISTORY_QUERY_ID. Cached privately — not committed.
